A co-authored **online after-class peer-learning platform** built around *sustainable
learning* — a peer-learning mechanism that lowers operating cost while students benefit
from the reciprocal effect of teaching and learning.

## Abstract

> This paper presents an online after-class learning platform that emphasizes sustainable
> learning, describing its underlying design principles, technical architecture, and
> implementation methods. Although initially designed for undergraduate students studied in
> computer science, the platform's functions and mechanisms have the potential to benefit
> students across various fields. The platform's design ingeniously utilizes a peer learning
> mechanism to reduce operating expense, achieving sustainability while enabling students to
> benefit from the reciprocal learning effect of teaching and learning. The development of
> this platform also incorporates the latest artificial intelligence and blockchain
> technologies to implement features such as a matching system, knowledge management system,
> and points mechanism. These technologies ensure the efficient operation of the peer learning
> mechanism, thereby enhancing overall learning effectiveness. The platform is currently under
> development, with the hope that once completed, it will have a positive impact on the field
> of E-learning.

## Highlights

- A **peer-learning mechanism** ("one for all, all for one") that reduces tutoring manpower and cost while improving outcomes through reciprocal teaching and learning.
- Integrates **artificial intelligence and blockchain** to power a **matching system**, a **knowledge-management system**, and a **points mechanism**.
- Designed first for CS undergraduates but extensible to other fields; aligned with **SDG #4 (Quality Education)**.
